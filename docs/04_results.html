Results, by test

Each test is reported in its own section, with the numbers taken verbatim from the corresponding report. Diagnostic results come first (Tests 1–2), then the generative attempts (Tests 3–4). The consolidated reading is deferred to §5.

4.1 Test 1 — block anomaly score (B3/B4) class B

In the focus state late_growth | tail_64_95 | even, the \(B_4\) score's actual median (\(-0.047\)) sits above the iid median (\(-0.060\)), and the weighted AUC separating high-scoring actual words from iid is \(0.719\). Within that state the lowest \(B_4\)-score decile has survival ratio \(S = 0.035\) and the highest decile \(S = 1.806\) — a clear monotone direction, on a thin cell.

Against the full logistic baseline, the block score adds a small but real amount of separation, and crucially does not absorb the structural covariates:

4.2 Test 2 — block-length renormalization (L = 3…6) class B

The report's self-verdict is B — "AUC grows but residual remains": longer blocks discriminate better but do not remove the bridge/parity structure.

4.3 Test 3 — finite-block reweighting class C

Reweighting iid words by \(2^{\alpha S_L}\) is the first generative attempt. The best full-state fit is barely-damped short blocks — \(L=3\), \(\alpha=0.25\), RMSE \(0.000440978\), JS \(0.000921815\). Longer or stronger reweighting is worse, not better: at \(L=6\) the best choice is \(\alpha = 0.0\) (no reweighting at all), RMSE \(0.000642649\).

The focus state shows the overcorrection directly. Its best reweighting (\(L=6\), \(\alpha=0.5\)) still under-predicts the mass — actual \(0.00092052\) vs predicted \(0.00029821\) — and pushing to \(\alpha=1\) collapses the modelled focus survival to \(0.00416667\) against an actual survival of \(0.472461\). At the global best fit the bridge RMSE is \(0.000427051\) and the parity RMSE \(0.00262763\).

The report's self-verdict is C — "reweighting overcorrects or is not generative": the small improvement is entirely from damped short-block reweighting; longer raw reweighting is over-sharp — useful diagnostically, weak as a generator.

4.4 Test 4 — maximum-entropy block projection class C

The second generative attempt replaces ad-hoc reweighting with an approximate regularized IPF that matches block marginals. It does not beat the simpler baseline: the best maximum-entropy fit (\(L=3\), regularization \(0.75\)) has RMSE \(0.000493147\) and JS \(0.000868073\), worse than the best raw/damped reweighting RMSE of \(0.000440978\).

Regularization does reduce the parity residual for the longer blocks — at regularization \(0\) the \(L=5\) and \(L=6\) parity residuals start high and fall toward the \(L=3/L=4\) level as regularization increases — but this is the projection being tamed toward the damped baseline, not a new structure being captured.

On the focus state the projection's best survival is \(0.444001\) (\(L=5\), regularization \(0.9\)) against an actual survival of \(0.472461\) — close in this one cell, but obtained at heavy regularization and not accompanied by a better global fit.

The report's self-verdict is C — "maxent no better than raw/damped", with the explicit reminder that this is an approximate projection, not a full exact IPF over all words.
